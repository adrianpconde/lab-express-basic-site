About The Mandalorian

The Mandalorian is a bounty hunter who was active during the early years of the New Republic Era.
Orphaned as a child when Separatist battle droids killed his parents, he was taken in by the Mandalorians and raised as a "found", orphan from another planet adopted and raised in their own culture, eventually becoming a member of a reclusive sect of Mandalorians, known as "the Tribe".
Due to his childhood trauma, the Mandalorian developed a notable hatred for droids. According to the tenets of the Tribe's Mandalorian Creed, the Mandalorian does not remove his helmet in the presence of others.
Over time, the Mandalorian became a member of the Bounty Hunters' Guild on Nevarro, developing a reputation of being an expensive but formidable bounty hunter.

Originally hired to capture Grogu, a light-green, Force-sensitive infant of the same species as Yoda –the well known character who appears on Star Wars franchise, from Episode I to Episode IV–, the Mandalorian instead protects him from a remnant of the fallen Galactic Empire, and becomes a father figure for him.
The dynamic between the Mandalorian and Grogu embodies a theme of parenting and fatherhood prevalent throughout the TV series.
